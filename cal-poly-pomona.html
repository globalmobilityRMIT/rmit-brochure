  • California Polytechnic Uni, Pomona (Cal Poly Pomona), offers top-quality academic programs in a range of fields. Faculty in all disciplines apply theory to practice, creating opportunities for students to use their knowledge in hands-on projects, collaborative research, internships and service learning programs. About 2,700 faculty and staff support the education of 22,000 students.
    Cal Poly Pomona is nestled in 1,438 rolling acres on the eastern edge of Los Angeles County. Due to its location at the nexus of Los Angeles, Orange and San Bernardino counties, students can engage in outdoor activities such as swimming in the ocean or skiing in the mountains.
    As one of only two polytechnic universities in California, Cal Poly Pomona is known for its learn-by-doing philosophy. The university offers more than 60 majors and degree programs in eight academic colleges.

  • Pomona is the fifth largest city in the sunny Los Angeles County, California. Pomona is recognised as a vibrant, safe and beautiful community that is home to artists, students, business and industry.

  • Cal Poly Pomona celebrated its 75th Anniversary in 2014.

Special requirements

Programs are taught in English.
Open to RMIT Undergraduate students.
Open to students from any compatible discipline.

For impacted majors, incoming exchange students to Pomona are required to have a GPA of 3.0 and above and are not guaranteed to take any specific courses.

Link for "impacted majors"- http://www.cpp.edu/~admissions/advising/impacted-majors.shtml?nav=undergrad

Engineering is limited and specific courses are not guaranteed

This institution has a quarter system. RMIT students are required to take minimum of 12 units per quarter but suggested to take 14-16 units or stay for 2 quarters if possible.
