  • Chonnam National University (CNU) in Korea was founded in 1952 and has grown to encompass 17 colleges and 10 graduate schools.
    Throughout its 50-year history, CNU has remained steadfast in its goals to foster the development and integrity of future leaders and professionals to become one of the world's leading institutions in research and scholarship.
    Over 160,000 students have graduated from CNU and the University currently has approximately 30,000 students and 1,500 faculty and staff members.

  • Gwangju has a population of 1.5 million and is the sixth largest city in Korea. The city was established in 57 BC, and has been a major political and economic centre in Korea ever since. 
    Gwangju has a thriving youth and arts culture, as evidenced by the on-going exhibitions and performances in the city.
    The city is famous for its rich and diverse cuisine. It plays host to the annual Gwangju Kimchi Festival that promotes the varieties of delicious Korean kimchi and traditional Korean culture.

  • CNU offers intensive Korean language courses throughout the semester free of charge to exchange students.

Living

Accommodation

On-campus accommodation is guaranteed for exchange students. Exchange students are automatically registered for an on-campus dormitory room after their application reaches CNU.

Health insurance

All incoming exchange students must purchase health insurance after their arrival at CNU.

Key information

Visa

Successful applicants will receive a ‘Certificate of Admission’ from CNU. Students are required to apply for a Student Visa (D-2) at their closest Korean embassy/consulate. As the processing of a visa takes several weeks, students are advised to apply for it immediately after receiving the certificate.
